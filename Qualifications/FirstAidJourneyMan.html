First Aid (Journeyman)

    Do 1 or 2:
  1. Complete a CPR-AED certification class taught by the American Heart Association or American Red Cross, or Emergency Care and Safety institute that includes skill practice with CPR dummies and a teaching AED and teaches these three skill sets: • Adult CPR, AED, and choking, • Child CPR, AED, and choking, and • Infant CPR and choking.
  2. Self-study
      Explain hands-only CPR and put on a mock performance of CPR in front of the troop.
      Explain the use of an AED and put on a mock Demonstration for the troop.
      Describe and demonstrate for the troop the positions for treating choking in an adult, pregnant woman, child, and infant.
    3. Do 3 or (5, 6 and 7):
  3. Complete a Basic First Aid certification class taught by the American Heart Association, American Red Cross, or Emergency Care and Safety Institute that includes a hands-on skills section.
  4. Self-study Option:
    1. Demonstrate first aid action plan basics:
        Checking the scene
        Calling for help
        Approaching safely
        Providing urgent treatment
        Treating for shock
        Deciding the next steps
        Demonstrate the following rescuer safety precautions:
        Wearing of eye protection, breathing mask, and vinyl gloves
        Proper glove removal
        Proper handwashing
  5. Why sun protection is important while on the water, in the snow, or at high altitude.
  6. Make first aid kits:
      List the items needed for a Hiking First Aid Kit, BackPacking First Aid Kit, Camping First Aid Kit
      Inventory or create your hiking first Aid kit and your back packing first aid kit to match your list.
      Inventory or create your guild's Camping First Aid Kit in accordance with your list and discussion with your guilg.
  7. Explain how to get assistance on a wilderness camping trip, river trip, and on open water.
  8. Demonstrate splinting, slings, and bandaging for:
      Twisted ankle
      Broken ankle
      Broken lower arm
      Broken upper arm
      Broken collarbone
      Broken lower leg
      Broken upper leg
  9. Emergency procedures for:
      Stop, Drop, and Roll
      another person who doesn't Stop Drop And Roll and panics and runs instead.
  10. Review the knowledge of two other troopers and second thier motion for qualification as an Apprentice before the court of honor. (Note: if you second a motion and the motion does not pass due to the lack of knowledge of the applicant you may risk losing your qualification).
  11. Select a trooper with Journeyman or better First Aid that is willing to review your knowledge and second your motion to the court of honor for your qualification. If no trooper is qualified this requirement can be waved by the Troop Master.
