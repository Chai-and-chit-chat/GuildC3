Training boys to be men.

The aim of the Boy Scouts is to supplement the various existing educational agencies, and to promote the ability in boys to do things for themselves and others. ... The method is summed up in the term Scoutcraft, and is a combination of observation, deduction, and handiness, or the ability to do things. Scoutcraft includes instruction in First Aid, Life Saving, Tracking, Signaling, Cycling, Nature Study, Seamanship, Campcraft, Woodcraft, Chivalry, Patriotism, and other subjects. This is accomplished in games and team play, and is pleasure, not work, for the boy. All that is needed is the out-of-doors, a group of boys, and a competent leader. - John L. Alexander - 1911

Our purpose today is little different from that of Mr. Alexander. Though over 100 years has passed since these words where written and much has changed in the world little has changed in the nature of boys. The Boy Scouts of America was a fantastic group at its origins it was designed to teach American boys the values and beliefs of the men and women that trained them.

Unfortunately over the last years a shadow has fallen over the organization because of the actions of a few that have disgraced the many. Boy Scouts of America has been discontinued and we have chosen not to associated with the new B.S.A. organization. We have instead decided to forge ahead independently on the foundation laid by Baden Powell in "Scouting for Boys" and blaze a new path designed for both boys of American Citizenship and boys of Foreign Nationalities or Heritage specifically that of South Asian. Though open to all boys the intent is to train the boys to understand not only America but their country of origin as well. So that they might understand the long heritage of the each countries fight for freedom against corruption and oppression that each nation represented has had to face.

For over 100 years men have measured their manhood by the 10 points of the scout law. The first list resembling the current form appeared in Baden-Powell's 1908 Scouting for Boys. The concepts and ideas contained in the scout law were used by Baden Powellin a post war Britain to train boys to act as scouts for the British millitary a fortuitous set of training given the number of boys that served in exactly that capacity in World War II. The 10th point of the Scout Law was added by Lord Powel in 1911.

Child Protection Policies

Unfortunately we live in a world gone mad and as such need to set guidelines to protect the young men that accompany us. Our first and prefered line of defense is that we prefer to have each boys father present at all activities but even in those cases the following rules apply. Any person observing a violation of these guidelines should address the issue by joining the group if needed and or report it immediantly.

1 Person Per Enclosure.

We are an outdoor community and we have few reasons to be inside an enclosure. When those times occur the enclosure is always lit and only one person can be in it at any time.

Partially in compliance with this rule when camping we use the ultra light setup of a ranger roll, Bivy or tarp and do not use enclosed tents.

2 Man Rule

Any interaction between an adult (18+) and boy (10-17) requires 2 adults to be involved.

Any over night event requires a minimum of 4 adults to be present. 2 to keep the 2 man rule with the troop and 2 to deal in concert with any emergency.

3 Boy Groups

An extension of the buddy system boys should always be in groups of 3. If a boy needs some time to step away it should be done in full visibility and without making the other members of his group violate the rule of 3.

This is perhaps the hardest of the 3 Guidelines to enforce boys run and rough house and enjoy games envolving hiding but these should be avoided.

Texas Department of State Health Services offers a list of certified training and examination programs at https://www.dshs.texas.gov/cpm/training.shtm
It is our expectation that at least 1 legal guardian of every boy will make a good faith attempt to complete this training and keep the certification up to date. Because it can be done online and only cost 10$ we recommend using https://safetysystem.ministrysafe.com/purchases/new but any of them would be acceptable. It takes about an hour to use Ministry Safe you'll want to just select the first option. "Sexual Abuse Awareness Training ($10)"
Warning this training can be a very disturbing glimpse into American Culture it may not be suitable for most minors. Please take the course so you can decide for yourself if your child should view it as well. Upon completeion please bring a copy of you certificate to a Court of Honor or have your son bring it to a meeting.
